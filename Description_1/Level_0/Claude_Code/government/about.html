City History

Lakewood's roots stretch back to the 1859 Gold Rush, when settlers established farms and ranches along the foothills of the Front Range. The area grew steadily through the early 20th century, with the Denver & Rio Grande Railroad bringing commerce and community.

On June 24, 1969, Lakewood was officially incorporated as a Home Rule Municipality — the first city in Colorado to do so — granting residents direct control over their local government through a city charter.

Through the 1970s and 80s, Lakewood transformed into a thriving suburb with a strong commercial corridor along Colfax Avenue and expansion westward toward the foothills. The opening of the RTD light rail W Line in 2013 connected Lakewood more directly to downtown Denver, spurring a new wave of urban development.

Today, Lakewood is recognized nationally for its commitment to open space preservation, with over 4,800 acres of parks and greenways, and ranks consistently among the best places to live in Colorado.

Mayor

Patricia Ramirez-Ortiz

Mayor Ramirez-Ortiz was elected in November 2021 and re-elected in 2023. She has championed affordable housing initiatives, the Lakewood Sustainability Plan, and investments in public safety. Before serving as Mayor, she was a Jefferson County educator and nonprofit leader for over 20 years.
